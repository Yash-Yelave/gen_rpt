{\textcolor{BOBlue}{\scriptsize\bfseries KEY FINDINGS}}\par\vspace{-1pt}
{\Large\sffamily\bfseries\color{BONavy} The evidence points to a small set of management implications}\par\vspace{4pt}
\vspace{2pt}{\color{BOBright}\rule{\linewidth}{1pt}}\vspace{5pt}
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{p{9mm}Y}
\textcolor{BOBlue}{\bfseries 01} & {\small\bfseries Fusion commercialization is at least 15-25 years away from grid-scale deployment.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Evidence: Public roadmaps from ITER (first plasma delayed to 2035), SPARC (targeting net energy gain by 2025-2030), and DEMO (2040s) indicate that commercial plants are unlikely before 2040-2050. Private ventures like Helion Energy target.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Management implication: Do not expect fusion to impact energy portfolios before 2040. Focus on near-term decarbonization options while preparing for fusion's potential long-term role.} \\[6pt]
\textcolor{BOBlue}{\bfseries 02} & {\small\bfseries Tritium supply is a critical bottleneck for fusion deployment.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Evidence: Current global tritium production is limited to about 0.5 kg/year from CANDU reactors, while a single fusion plant may require 0.1-0.2 kg/day. Breeding blankets are not yet demonstrated at scale.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Management implication: Invest in tritium supply chain development and monitor breeding blanket demonstrations. Companies securing tritium sources may gain strategic advantage.} \\[6pt]
\textcolor{BOBlue}{\bfseries 03} & {\small\bfseries Fusion's levelized cost of energy (LCOE) is highly uncertain but likely higher than renewables for the foreseeable future.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Evidence: Projections range from \$50-100/MWh for mature fusion (post-2050) to over \$200/MWh for first-of-a-kind plants. Solar and wind LCOE are already below \$30/MWh in many regions, and battery storage costs are falling rapidly.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Management implication: Fusion will not compete on cost alone. Its value lies in firm, dispatchable, carbon-free power. Position fusion as a complement to renewables, not a replacement.} \\[6pt]
\textcolor{BOBlue}{\bfseries 04} & {\small\bfseries Regulatory frameworks for fusion are nascent and vary by jurisdiction.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Evidence: The US Nuclear Regulatory Commission is developing a framework for fusion, but no licenses have been issued. The UK and EU are also working on regulatory pathways. Uncertainty could delay deployment by 5-10 years.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Management implication: Engage with regulators early to shape licensing requirements. Early movers in regulatory sandboxes may gain time-to-market advantages.} \\[6pt]
\textcolor{BOBlue}{\bfseries 05} & {\small\bfseries Investment in fusion is growing rapidly but carries high risk.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Evidence: Private fusion investment reached \$2.8 billion in 2022, with major investors including Bill Gates, Jeff Bezos, and venture capital firms. However, no fusion startup has yet demonstrated net energy gain at scale.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Management implication: Diversify fusion investments across multiple approaches and stages. Use phased commitments to manage risk, and consider co-investment with government programs.} \\[6pt]
\textcolor{BOBlue}{\bfseries 06} & {\small\bfseries Geopolitical implications include reduced dependence on fossil fuel exporters and new supply chain dependencies.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Evidence: Countries with advanced fusion programs (US, China, UK, EU) may gain energy export advantages. Fusion fuel (tritium, lithium) and advanced materials (beryllium, niobium-tin) create new supply chain vulnerabilities.}\par{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Management implication: Monitor geopolitical developments and diversify supply chains for critical materials. Consider strategic partnerships with leading fusion nations.} \\[6pt]
\end{tabularx}

{\color{BOBlue}\sffamily\bfseries CEO Decision Scenario: To Invest or Wait in Fusion?}\par\vspace{2pt}
{\small The CEO of a major energy company is considering a \$500 million investment in a leading fusion startup that promises first power by 2028. The startup has demonstrated promising plasma confinement results but has not yet achieved net energy gain. Competitors are also investing in fusion, and the company's board is divided on the risk-reward profile.}\par
{\small\textbf{CEO question:} Should we commit significant capital now to secure a first-mover advantage, or wait for clearer technical and regulatory milestones?}\par
{\small\textbf{Recommended move:} Adopt a phased investment approach: commit \$50 million for a minority stake with board observation rights, and tie further funding to specific milestones (e.g., net energy gain demonstration, regulatory approval). This limits downside while maintaining strategic optionality.}\par
{\scriptsize\color{BOMuted} Avoid overpaying for hype; ensure the startup has a credible path to commercialization beyond 2028. Monitor competitor moves and regulatory developments closely. Be prepared to exit if milestones are missed.}\par\vspace{8pt}
