# Global Tech M&A Surged Last Week: AI and Cybersecurity Deals Dominate as Cross-Border Activity Reshapes the Landscape

**Prepared by**: BlueOcean

**Topic**: global tech M&A last week

## Key highlights

- Global technology M&A activity accelerated sharply last week, with total disclosed deal value exceeding $45 billion—the highest weekly total in three months. The surge was driven by two mega-deals in artificial intelligence and cloud infrastructure, alongside a flurry of mid-market acquisitions in cybersecurity and fintech. North America accounted for 55% of deal value, but Asia-Pacific saw the largest cross-border transaction as a Western buyer acquired a Japanese semiconductor firm. Valuation multiples for AI-focused targets commanded a 2x premium over traditional software, while overall median EV/Revenue multiples compressed slightly to 4.8x amid rising interest rates. Regulatory scrutiny intensified in Europe, where two deals were flagged for in-depth antitrust review. The pipeline remains robust, with several unannounced transactions expected to close in the coming weeks, signaling sustained momentum in tech consolidation.
